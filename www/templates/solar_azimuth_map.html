The most interesting example is Stonehenge (lat: 51:10:43.84, longitude: -1:49:34.28). Setting the date to the summer solstice (June 21) and assuming prehistoric Britons were not using daylight saving time, this gives a sunrise azimuth of 49:54 which is in good agreement with what I found on this site and lines up with what we see in the satellite image with google maps (left). Conversely, sun set on the winter solstice (December 21) is the reverse of this.
